Predicting trends in the music industry by analysing the sentiment of tweets with machine learning algorithms.


The internet has changed the way in which we consume and share music. Streaming has caused the music industry to grow for the first time since 2015 after steep decline due to digital file sharing and piracy (PSTH, 2021). As a result, the music industry has seen a 13% compound annual growth rate (CAGR) since 2015 (PSTH, 2021).


Predicting listening trends has historically been difficult as there is no single determinant of success (name recognition vs sound, seasonality, marketing; Georgieva et al. 2018). New, digital music ecosystem increases access to data and has enabled labels to conduct data-driven decision making and thus reduce risk associated with investment decisions.


'Sentiment analysis or opinion mining is the computational study of people's opinions, sentiments, emotions, appraisals, and attitudes towards entities such as products, services, organisations, individuals, issues, events, topics, and their attributes' (Liu, 2015; Zhang et al., 2017). Sentiment analysis can be used to automatically collect information about user preferences. In order to do this, natural language processing (NLP) must be utilised to give meaning to the mined data.


Studies to date have primarily focused on predicting which songs will become 'hits' based on the volume of positive sentiment within a sample (Kim et al., 2014). Araujo et al. (2017) found that the volume of positive tweets (one month pre-album release) can account for a 95.5% variation in popularity on Spotify.


At present, trend prediction studies using twitter data give every tweet equal weight, irrespective of the tweeter's following. However, social media influencers play an important role in the marketing ecosystem given their large followings and reach. Using the analytical hierarchy process we can weight the value of tweets based on the poster's following and potentially improve the accuracy of current sentiment analysis models.
