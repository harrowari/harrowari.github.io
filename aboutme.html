About me


'An ambitious and driven individual with an interest in using technology to help businesses face challenges and solve problems. I have four year's coding experience, both academic and professional. I enjoy working towards innovative solutions and being exposed to new problems. I hope to work in a friendly and collaborative environment as a part of a high performing team.'


There’s an incredible sense of satisfaction and achievement in finding innovative solutions to problems and processes which are considered too challenging or cumbersome. This is what led me to the study of computer science and programming. During my undergraduate degree I had completed a topographic survey of a beach to assess flooding risk for houses in the area. The transects needed to be plotted for use in a report and were generally displayed as line graphs displayed distance and height. I felt that this approach was substandard and our data could be better displayed as a 3D surface plot by interpolating between transects. At this point I had never written a line of code before but was determined to produce what I had envisioned. Over the next four weeks I became obsessed with learning the tools I needed to derive the finished product. By the end of this task I was hooked on programming and sought to incorporate as much into my undergraduate degree as possible, despite it not being featured in the syllabus.


I was lucky enough to work with a fantastic supervisor for my undergraduate dissertation who used cloud computing to produce global models of dust emissions into the atmosphere. Over the next six months I learnt more than I could ever have imagined - a testament to the value of great mentors. A keen interest in scientific and numerical modelling was also borne. Attempting to describe complex, natural processes is an often imperfect task given the sheer number of variables at play. It requires endless iteration and refinement of assumptions. I found a certain beauty in seeing my model progress from being incredibly primitive to a point where it could actually describe the world around me.


After graduating I worked for a local council’s planning department where I provided GIS support. I gained a good knowledge of the UK planning system and property market while also being exposed to large-scale, corporate IT and computing systems.


Wanting to build on my education and experiences to date I undertook an MSc in Computing at Cardiff University. This gave me the opportunity to build on the knowledge of the last three years, leaving me with a more comprehensive understanding of the field of computing.


Alongside my experience in the planning industry, I have always been interested in economics, business and finance and as a result have sought to learn as much as possible. It just so happens that these industries utilise many of the technologies and methods I became passionate about during my undergraduate degree. I have decided to seek employment in fintech and/or proptech as I believe the work would prove both fulfilling and intellectually stimulating.
